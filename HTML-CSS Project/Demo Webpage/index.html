Extended reality (XR) is a term that refers to a range of immersive technologies, including augmented reality (AR), virtual reality (VR), and mixed reality (MR). XR is a concept that combines or mirrors the physical world with a digital twin world. It aims to provide users with an immersive experience by being in a virtual or augmented environment. XR is changing the way people work, live, and play. XR is revolutionizing everyday consumer experiences and transforming many industry segments, including industrial manufacturing, healthcare, education, and retail.

Augmented Reality

Augmented reality (AR) is an interactive experience that enhances the real world with computer-generated visual elements, sounds, and other stimuli. AR is a combination of the real scene viewed by the user and a virtual scene generated by the computer.

Vertual Reality

Virtual reality (VR) is a computer-generated, three-dimensional environment that users can interact with in real time. VR creates an immersive experience that can help users visualize and understand complex concepts, simulate human experiences, and explore virtual worlds.

Mixed Reality

Mixed reality (MR) is a technology that combines virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR). It lets users interact with and manipulate both physical and virtual items and environments.
